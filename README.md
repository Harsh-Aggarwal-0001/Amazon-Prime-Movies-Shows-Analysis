# Amazon-Prime-Movies-Shows-Analysis
This Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) capstone project investigates the content landscape of Amazon Prime using a dataset of over 9,800 movies and TV shows. In a competitive streaming market, understanding catalog dynamics and viewer reception is vital for sustaining subscription growth and optimizing budgets. The primary objective is to uncover underlying patterns and audience preferences to provide data-driven recommendations for future content strategies.

After rigorous data wrangling—including handling missing values and merging datasets—several key insights emerged. The platform's catalog is heavily skewed toward US-produced, adult-oriented movies, exposing a gap in family-friendly and international programming. Furthermore, audience ratings (IMDb and TMDB) cluster around the 6.0–7.0 mark, indicating a stable but "average" baseline with a lack of highly-rated premium blockbuster content. Crucially, while movies dominate in sheer volume, TV shows exhibit slightly higher median ratings but suffer from a drastic drop-off rate after their first season.

To drive user engagement and reduce churn, the platform should pivot toward developing high-quality, multi-season TV series that guarantee long-term viewership. Additionally, diversifying the catalog with international productions and family-friendly programming will help unlock new demographic segments and ensure sustainable business growth.
